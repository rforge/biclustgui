Spectral Biclustering

Spectral biclustering (Kluger et al., 2003) assumes that the matrices of gene expression data contain a checkerboard structure after normalization. The method clusters the rows (genes) and columns (conditions) simultaneously by identifying the checkerboard structure using eigenvector computations. The spectral biclustering produces non overlap biclusters with coherent values.
